BIOGRAPHY

Grooverider began DJing on the London pirate station Phase One in the mid-1980s, initially playing soul, hip hop and disco before moving into house music. With Fabio, he became central to the transition from acid house and breakbeat hardcore into jungle and drum & bass.

The pair’s residency at Rage at Heaven became one of the scene’s defining laboratories. During the 1990s, Grooverider pushed darker, tougher sounds, founded Prototype Recordings, released music under aliases including Codename John, and helped introduce artists such as Matrix and Boymerang to a wider audience.

By the late 1990s his influence extended through The Prototype Years, the album Mysteries of Funk, international touring and national radio with Fabio. This profile documents his activity up to 2005.
